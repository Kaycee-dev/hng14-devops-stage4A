## What I can defend without AI

- **Why `up -d --no-deps --force-recreate app` and not `restart`**: `restart`
  reuses the existing container with its baked env; we need the new MODE env
  from the regenerated compose file. `up -d` recreates on config change,
  `--no-deps` keeps nginx alive, `--force-recreate` handles canary->canary
  where compose would otherwise see no diff.
- **Why poll through nginx, not directly to the app**: end-to-end health is
  what we mean by "deployed." Hitting the app port directly would tell us the
  app is up but not that the proxy is routing. Brief also forbids exposing the
  app port, so the host port we have IS nginx's.
- **Why two independent signals to confirm mode** (body.mode AND X-Mode
  header): if X-Mode middleware is ever broken, body.mode still tells the
  truth. Defense in depth at the contract level.
- **Why a targeted regex for the manifest mode flip, not yaml.safe_dump**:
  `safe_dump` rewrites the whole file and strips comments/blank lines/quoting.
  The regex `(?m)^(\s*mode:[ \t]+)\S+` changes exactly one line. Plus a
  post-edit `yaml.safe_load` sanity check, so a malformed write fails loudly.
- **Why `teardown -v` is on by default**: without `-v`, the named log volume
  persists across teardown, surprising the next deploy with old logs. Brief
  says teardown removes "containers, networks, and volumes" — `-v` is the
  literal flag for the third one.
- **Why `--clean` is opt-in for deleting generated configs**: most operators
  want to teardown the running stack between iterations without losing the
  rendered files (avoids re-running init). The `--clean` flag is the
  "back to source-only" mode the grader will use.
